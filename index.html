THE TRAP 1 — ablative, NOT dative
-o (sg) and -is (pl) can be dative OR ablative. After a preposition it is ALWAYS ablative.
After cum (with), in (in/on), e/ex (out of), a/ab (from) → ablative. So cum amico = ablative, in villis = ablative. In the exam these were marked dative — they are ablative.
✍ COPY THIS INTO YOUR BOOK
THE TRAP 2 — adjectives agree; translate BOTH words
An adjective copies its noun’s case and number. Translate the noun AND the adjective, and keep the plural. Beware the English look-alikes.
Latinlooks like…but really means
laetae‘late’happy
miseri‘mischievous’wretched / miserable
nautae‘nature’sailors
magnum‘magnum’big / large
So puellae laetae = the happy girls (plural), nautae miseri = wretched sailors.
